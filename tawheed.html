What is Tawhid?

Tawhid means the oneness and uniqueness of Allah in His Lordship, Worship, and Names and Attributes. It is the cornerstone of the Islamic faith and is the message that all Prophets brought to their people.

Shar’i Definition of Tawhid

In Shari’ah, Tawhid is defined as singling out Allah in worship and recognizing His oneness in His Lordship and Names and Attributes. This is the essence of the testimony of faith: La ilaha illallah (There is no deity worthy of worship except Allah).

Categories of Tawheed

The concept of Tawheed can be categorized into three parts:

  • Tawheed al-Rububiyyah
  • Tawheed al-Uluhiyyah
  • Tawhid al-Asma wa’l-Sifat

What is Tawheed al-Rububiyyah?

This is the belief in the oneness of Allah’s Lordship, acknowledging that He alone is the Creator, Sustainer, Provider, and Controller of all that exists. Allah says:

اللّٰهُ خَالِقُ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ

"Allah is the Creator of all things."

Surah Az-Zumar (39:62)

What is Tawheed al-Uluhiyyah?

Tawheed al-Uluhiyyah refers to singling out Allah in all acts of worship, such as prayer, fasting, supplication, and reliance. Worshipping anything or anyone besides Allah is considered shirk. Allah says:

وَاعْبُدُوا اللَّهَ وَلَا تُشْرِكُوا بِهِ شَيْئًا

"And worship Allah and associate nothing with Him."

Surah An-Nisa (4:36)

What is Tawhid al-Asma wa’l-Sifat?

This entails affirming all the names and attributes of Allah as mentioned in the Quran and Sunnah, without distorting, denying, or likening them to creation. Allah says:

لَيْسَ كَمِثْلِهِ شَيْءٌ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ

"There is nothing like unto Him, and He is the Hearing, the Seeing."

Surah Ash-Shura (42:11)

Principles of Tawhid al-Asma wa’l-Sifat

The principles of Tawhid al-Asma wa’l-Sifat include:

  • Affirming all attributes of Allah mentioned in the Quran and Sunnah.
  • Negating any imperfections or deficiencies in Allah.
  • Believing that His attributes are unique and incomparable.
